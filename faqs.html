Rekeying involves changing the internal mechanism of a lock so that old keys no longer work. It’s a great option if you've lost your keys or moved into a new home and want to ensure previous keys won’t grant access. It’s often more affordable than a full lock change.

What is the difference between a lock change and rekeying?

A lock change involves replacing the entire lock system, while rekeying changes the internal mechanism of the lock so that the old keys no longer work. Rekeying is often a more affordable option if you want to keep the existing hardware but ensure only new keys can open the lock.
